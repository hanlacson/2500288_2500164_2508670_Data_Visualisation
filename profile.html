Nancy Wake

Nancy Grace Augusta Wake, she was born in 1912 on the 30th of August in Wellington, New Zealand and she sadly passed in 2011, she was known in the SOE force as the White Mouse, she was just a journalist when the second world war started and she quickly assigned herself into the French Resistance Force with her role being a courier and also a saboteur against the axis. She was known for her amazing ability of escaping and evading the German police. Her most notable acts were the missions she completed for example after she left London she parachuted into France to lead and assist the Maquis fighters, New Zealand-born Australian agent known as the "White Mouse." She helped thousands of Allied soldiers escape from France and later led guerrilla attacks against the Nazis as part of the French Resistance.

Noor Inayat Khan

Noor Un Nisa Inayat Khan, she was a brave SOE agent born in 1914 from Moscow Russia and was given the codename Madeline in the military, her background is incredibly interesting as she was a descendant of Indian royalty and brought up with the ideology of being a pacifist. When she joined the SOE in order to serve freedom she was first trained up to be a wireless operator, which was deemed as one of the most dangerous roles in the whole SOE. One of her biggest accomplishments whilst being in the force was that she was the first female radio communications operator to be sent into the dangerous Nazi occupied France, and it was said that she kept up strong communications for seven months. But she was eventually found by the ruthless Gestapo and then captured where she would then be tortured and chained up for ten months before she was then executed, she remained strong the whole time and never gave the Nazi's any information and she will be remembered as a heroine and a true patriot to freedom.

Violette Szabo

Violette Reine Elizabeth Szabo,she was born 1921 in Paris France but raised in London, during the conflict her husband was killed fighting in north Africa which then motivated her to help the war effort by joining the SOE branch, she was trained in the ways of sabotage and parachuting an would carry this out in the field where she was dropped into france from and aeroplane twice with the second time being very shortly after the infamous D-day attack, during her second deployment she led an intense firefight against the germans, which ended in her group escaping to safety but she was not so lucky and was also caught by the Gestapo where she was then take to Ravensbruck concentration camp where she endured inhumane treatment and conditions, but sadly she was brutally executed by the Germans,she was an incredibly strong woman who never gave the germans any intelligence and was awarded with one of the highest of achievements in England being the George Cross posthumously and she will be remembered for all of time for her bravery.
